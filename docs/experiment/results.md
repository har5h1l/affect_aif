## Current Top-Line Read

Affective precision is behaviorally active on the supported architecture, but
the result is conditional rather than globally payoff-improving.

- H0: policy-space openness gates visible affect effects.
- H1: precision tracks model fitness/predictive reliability more than reward.
- H2: affect changes deployment even when partner inference is preserved.
- H3: stress reveals a boundary condition where precision can sharpen wrong
  deployment and hurt payoff.
- H4: partner choice changes before payoff necessarily moves.
- H5: perturbations separate mainly in beta dynamics and behavior; payoff is
  underpowered in the five-seed runs.

## Archive Policy

Older material that used C-condition labels, reward-average prototypes, Stag
Hunt/Chicken comparisons, pre-native variational-beta prototypes, or the
pre-cutover custom engine is historical context only. Do not use those numbers
as current claims unless the corresponding experiment is rerun on the supported
`inferactively-pymdp==1.0.0` trust-task architecture and documented under
`docs/results/runs/`.
