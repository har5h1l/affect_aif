## Expected Findings

- Policy openness matters. Graded choice has high policy entropy, and affect
  lowers entropy while improving payoff.
- Lesion and affect separate behaviorally in the open graded regime while belief
  accuracy remains broadly similar.
- Partner-specific precision changes social choice even when total payoff is
  flat.
- Clinical-like variants produce meaningful phenotype differences in beta range,
  policy entropy, partner-selection entropy, and payoff ordering.
- `alpha_charge` changes beta movement in the expected direction.

## Unexpected Findings

- H3 is not a clean affect win. In graded betrayal, affect lowers entropy but
  hurts total payoff by -73.6 relative to no-affect/lesion.
- H1 needed targeted analysis rather than payoff comparison. The added
  partner-level diagnostic supports reliability-over-reward, but only as pilot
  evidence because the run has five seeds.
- Blunted/alexithymia-like dynamics can outperform default affect in betrayal,
  suggesting that inertia is protective under this specific switch design.
- The default `alpha_charge=3.0` is not the payoff optimum in the stress
  sensitivity sweep.

## Hypothesis Implications

- **H0:** Supported, with the sharper claim that openness is necessary but not
  sufficient. Open policy spaces let affect move behavior; they do not guarantee
  affect helps.
- **H1:** Supported as pilot evidence. Precision tracks surprise more strongly
  than realized partner payoff, but this needs higher-rep confirmation.
- **H2:** Supported in the graded open regime. This is currently the strongest
  "knowing versus using" evidence.
- **H3:** Mixed boundary condition. The current betrayal scenario appears to
  measure overconfident wrong-belief deployment more than recovery.
- **H4:** Supported behaviorally. Payoff-flat partner choice is still useful
  evidence because the predicted readout is selection distribution and entropy.
- **H5:** Supported as a phenotype-dynamics claim; payoff confirmation needs
  higher replication and possibly separate stress-task calibration.
