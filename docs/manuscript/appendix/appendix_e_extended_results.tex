\section{Extended Profile and Robustness Results}
\label{app:extended_results}

This appendix reports extended parameter sweeps, profile analyses, and robustness diagnostics that support the main-text results.

\subsection{Precision-dynamics parameter variation}
\label{sec:perturbation_dynamics}

The following auxiliary perturbation controls vary the amplitude and stability of $\beta_k$ dynamics in the graded decision regime. They support interpretation of the main-text deployment results but are not themselves core empirical claims. Flattening the precision signal produces the clearest behavioral change. The low-gain variant compresses $\beta_k$ range to $0.24$ and raises entropy to $8.9$, near the no-affect and tracked-only baselines. Expanded-dynamics variants produce wider $\beta_k$ movement, but payoff does not follow range monotonically. The parameter sweeps below therefore vary gain and prior structure directly.

\subsection{Gain-prior profile signatures}
\label{sec:phenotypes_quadrants}

Crossing prior model fitness with precision gain defines the profile variants used in Section~\ref{sec:phenotypes}. ``Naive'' and ``cautious'' refer to the initial prior over $q(\beta_k)$ on support $\{0.5, 0.67, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0\}$: naive uses $[0.40, 0.40, 0.15, 0.04, 0.01]$, while cautious uses $[0.01, 0.04, 0.15, 0.40, 0.40]$. Low and high gain refer to $\alpha=0.1$ and $\alpha=3.0$, respectively, with the default agent using $\alpha=3.0$ and centered initialization at $\beta_k=1.0$. $\beta_k$ range denotes the mean within-episode range of the inverse-precision estimate. \subsection{Forgiveness and trust repair}

The forgiveness paradigm uses a 200-round partner-choice episode: cooperative baseline (rounds 1--80), betrayal on P0 (rounds 81--120), then reversion to cooperative/trusting behavior (rounds 121--200). Readouts include reengagement latency, post-return selection, payoff recovery, and $\beta_k$ recovery trajectories for the reverted partner.
