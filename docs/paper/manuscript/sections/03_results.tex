\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

\paragraph{Current evidence status.}
The current implementation uses Hesp-style partner-action surprisal,
$\epsilon_k=-\log P(o_k^{\mathrm{act}})$, with
$\sigma_0^2=(-\log 0.5)^2$ as the neutral binary-prediction baseline. The
numerical results below are a reduced three-seed post-fix rebaseline smoke
(\texttt{log\_surprisal\_spine\_smoke\_postfix\_20260528}). This run was
launched after tracing and fixing an agent-choice candidate-aggregation
artifact in the beta-to-gamma deployment path: directly scaling negative
policy scores by a lower gamma could make uncertain branches more selectable
by shrinking their scores toward zero. The runtime now uses centered precision
logits for cross-partner candidate comparison. The results below are current
smoke evidence, but they are not final publication evidence until replaced by
confirmation-scale reruns.

\subsection{The model-fitness claim remains unresolved}
\label{sec:h1}

The model-fitness test asks whether the precision signal follows predictive
reliability rather than realized reward. In the reduced reliability-versus-
reward smoke, the local affective variant does not preserve this dissociation:
$|\mathrm{corr}(\mathrm{precision},\mathrm{surprise})|=0.226$ versus
$|\mathrm{corr}(\mathrm{precision},\mathrm{payoff})|=0.615$ across
partner-seed units. The shared-global tracker is weak in the same diagnostic
($0.115$ versus $0.103$). Thus the post-fix H1 smoke does not currently
support a surprise-over-reward model-fitness claim.

The behavioral readout is not a payoff win. Mean total payoff is $492.7$ for
local affect, $552.0$ for no affect, and $512.7$ for global beta over three
seeds. This makes H1 a confirmation/rework item rather than a current
manuscript anchor. The theory still motivates partner-model fitness precision,
but the post-fix smoke does not yet establish that interpretation empirically.

\subsection{Open policy spaces show deployment changes, not a stable payoff gain}
\label{sec:h2}

The open graded-choice smoke tests whether precision has room to affect policy
deployment. Local affect lowers policy entropy relative to no affect
($8.59$ versus $8.79$), confirming that the beta-to-gamma pathway is active.
However, the payoff ordering does not reproduce the older bounded-error
advantage: local affect reaches $1851.3$, no affect or the tracked-only lesion
reaches $1864.2$, and global beta reaches $1851.3$ with high seed-to-seed
variance.

The matching H2 lesion run deliberately reuses this open-regime setup and adds
tracked-only and no-epistemic controls. In this reduced run, the tracked-only
lesion is numerically identical to no affect on payoff and entropy, as expected
when beta is tracked but decoupled from policy precision. The local affect
variant separates in entropy but not in payoff. For the current manuscript this
should be written as evidence that precision changes deployment in an open
policy space, not as evidence that local affect reliably improves reward.

\subsection{Partner-local and global beta separate signal quality from behavior}
\label{sec:h3}

The focal-switch locality probe separates interpretability from aggregate
performance. Local beta has the cleanest partner-specific diagnostic:
$|\mathrm{corr}(\mathrm{precision},\mathrm{surprise})|=0.943$ versus
$|\mathrm{corr}(\mathrm{precision},\mathrm{payoff})|=0.110$. Global beta has a
much weaker but still surprise-dominant association ($0.149$ versus $0.043$).
No affect and tracked-only match exactly on payoff and accuracy, which is the
expected control pattern when precision tracking is decoupled from action.

Aggregate payoff does not establish a locality advantage in this smoke:
global beta, local beta, and no affect are close ($976.2$, $946.8$, and
$950.7$). The correct interpretation is therefore an open decomposition:
partner-local beta is the cleaner relationship-specific readout,
whereas the current task has not shown that locality is behaviorally necessary.

\subsection{Abrupt volatility is repaired at smoke scale}
\label{sec:betrayal}

The betrayal-choice smoke is the most important repaired signal. Under the
centered selector, local affect outperforms the no-affect and tracked-only
controls at smoke scale: total payoff $1322.3$ versus $1225.0$, with lower
policy entropy ($7.47$ versus $8.68$) and lower joint accuracy
($0.319$ versus $0.425$). The result is too small for publication on its own,
but it reverses the pre-fix warning and makes H5 the strongest candidate
positive behavioral anchor for confirmation.

The repaired pattern still matters mechanistically because the payoff gain is
not driven by better final joint accuracy. Instead, the precision channel
appears to alter deployment under abrupt social volatility in a way that is
reward-beneficial despite imperfect belief labels. This should be tested next
with confirmation-scale seeds and post-switch trajectory summaries before it is
used as a manuscript claim.

\subsection{Social allocation and perturbation results remain supplemental}
\label{sec:pheno}

The H4 partner-choice smoke is too small for a payoff claim. Local affect,
global beta, and no affect are close in aggregate reward ($377.3$, $388.7$,
and $385.3$), while local affect again lowers entropy relative to no affect
($4.66$ versus $4.81$). This is consistent with deployment moving before
reward becomes informative, but it is not manuscript-ready evidence by itself.

The H6 perturbation smoke validates the intended precision-dynamics direction:
alexithymia-like parameters strongly damp beta movement (mean range $0.180$),
whereas borderline-like and depression-like settings broaden beta movement
($1.412$ and $1.464$). Payoff and accuracy differences are modest and
underpowered. These variants should remain supplemental computational
perturbations, not clinical validation claims.

\subsection{Publication-readiness implication}
\label{sec:publication-readiness}

The post-fix smoke preserves the diagnostic value of the H0--H6 spine, but it
does not yet make the paper publication-ready. The confirmation plan should be
narrow: first, test H5 as the repaired behavioral anchor; second, rerun or
revise H1 before using it as evidence for partner-model fitness; third, treat
H0, H2, and H4 as deployment/entropy support unless higher-seed reruns
establish stable payoff effects. H3 and H6 should remain decomposition and
perturbation analyses rather than necessity or clinical-validation claims.
